Who Am I

I am a Third year Electrical Electronic Engineering student pursuing a MEng degree at Imperial College London. Throughout my years at Imperial College I have been involved in various projects and an internship, more details of which can be found on the Experience/Projects sections below My fields of interest are mainly Biomedical hardware development and front-end software development

This Portfolio is created in order to give an overview of my academic and professional career and it serves as an extension to my CV.

Experience

Kouo Ltd

Hardware Engineer

Heart sensor project

  • Researched and purchased a number of ECG and PPG sensors for testing. Decided on the best over a range of metrics.
  • Once the sensor was selected I worked in C++ to get the heartrate, then I worked in Python on Pycharm to develop a breathing rate algorithm.
  • By the end of the summer I had integrated the sensor into Kouo's existing hardware.

Sweat sensor project

  • Similarly to the previous project I researched and purchased a number of sweat detecting sensors.
  • All the sensors were tested on a number of metrics such as sensitivity, to test them I recorded their response whilst relaxing and also whilst sweating.
  • I collated the results of the project into a report.

Material testing

  • I tested a number of materials to observe their efficacy as EEG electrodes. To carry out this testing I used a wavegenerator to simulate the EEG signal the brain would emit and amplified the signal after it had passed through the material undertest so it could be observed.
  • All the materials were tested on a number of metrics such as frequency response and amplitude.
  • I collated the results of the project into a report.

Projects

EE Rover

The final project of my first year was to build a rover with a range of sensors to identify a number of lizards based on the signals they outputted. The signals outputted included infrared, radio and magnetic. My responsibilities included the motor circuit and EM wave sensor. Leading on developing sensory approach and motor integration helped me to develope project management skills. I also improved my technical skills.

Pill dispenser project

At the end of my second year I had a group project in which we were asked to solve a socioeconomic problem, my group chose ’poorly followed prescriptions’. We built a device alongside an app that would help people to follow their prescriptions with features such as highlighting medications that shouldn't be taken together. I designed and tested the ultrasound sensor system through which developing debugging skills. I also worked on code for the motors used for dispensing this improved my C++ coding skills.

Issie project

As part of my third year module High Level Programming I had a group project in which we were set the task of coding a replacement software on which the first years could learn digital electronics. I coded the symbols and the positioning of their ports, I developed integration skills through working in a team. I developed FSharp coding skills writing code throughout the project.

Minexx project

As the end of year group project in my third year we created a security token and marketing website for Minexx. I worked on our leaflet and presentation outlining Minexx’s work and our part within it. This helped me to develope marketing and pitching skills. I also worked on the code of the website, in turn improving my HTML and CSS coding skills.
